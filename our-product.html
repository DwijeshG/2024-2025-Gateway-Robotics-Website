The Design

The robot is designed with various features to efficiently perform tasks on the course. It fouses on the theme for this year which is a moon rover. It has four wheels-two powered in the front and two passive in the back-which allow for easy maneuverability. A wall-like attachment on the front helps in pushing items, while a dump truck is utilized to transport and distribute materials. The cockpit serves as a seating area for the astronaut, while the robotics team operates the robot's movements using a controller. Additionally, there is an antenna arm that assists in pushing the button to adjust the antenna.

Our plan on the Moon

The competition consists of several tasks for the robot. In the "Robot Self-Preservation" challenge, the robot must leave the starting area and then return, earning 15 points. In the "Astronaut Stability Challenge", an astronaut figure is placed on the robot, which must remain balanced for the entire round with its head above its waist, providing approximately 50 points; this figure must be secured at the start of the round and cannot move until the end. For the "Antenna Repair", the robot is required to press a button to repair its antenna, allowing the spotter to track it more effectively, earning 15 points through a mechanism that uses a PVC pipe with an extension for reliable button pressing. In the "Habitat Modules - Dump Truck Mechanism" task, the robot must carry and dump habitat modules into designated spots using a dump truck mechanism, which features a bumper-like design for precise positioning of the boxes. Finally, the "Hoberman Sphere" task allows only one team to score per round by activating the sphere located in the center of the arena; although point values are not specified, it is anticipated to be a high score since only one team can score each round, with activation possible through the robot's bumper mechanism if they reach it first.
